About Daily Hit Metrics

Daily Hit Metrics is a real-time internet pulse tracker that aggregates trending content from across the web and presents it in a single, clean dashboard — updated every hour, around the clock.

What We Track

Our platform monitors six categories of trending activity across the internet:

World News — Breaking and developing stories from The Guardian, BBC News, NPR, and Al Jazeera, covering major global events as they unfold.

Social Pulse — The most engaging discussions happening on Reddit's most active communities, tracking viral conversations and mass social engagement in real time.

Tech — Top stories from Hacker News and Ars Technica, reflecting what the technology and startup community is reading and discussing.

Video Games — The latest from IGN and the r/gaming community, covering releases, announcements, and discussions in the gaming world.

Finance Trends — Active market discussions from retail investor communities on Reddit, covering stocks, options, macroeconomics, and trading activity.

Popular Searches — What people around the world are searching for right now, broken down by continent using Google Trends data from the United States, North America, South America, Europe, Africa, Asia, and Oceania.

How It Works

Our automated data pipeline runs every hour, pulling from publicly available RSS feeds and official APIs across dozens of sources. The data is processed, ranked by engagement and velocity, and published to our dashboard without human editorial intervention. What you see is what the internet is paying attention to, right now.
